Divvy

Synopsis

This is Divvy's bike-share analysis case study. In this case study, I will be following the steps of the data analysis process: ask, prepare, process, analyze, share, and act; in order to answer the key business questions.

Technologies Used

  • Microsoft Excel for changing file format from .xlsx to .csv.
  • ‘R’ Studio to upload and handle multiple large data frames.
  • Libraries like 'tidyverse' and 'lubridate' to wrangle, clean, process and analyze dataframes.
  • 'ggplot2' for data visualization and dashboards.
  • some library to export certain tables.
  • tableau for dashboard viz.

3 Questions that will guide future marketing progam:

  1. How do annual members and casual riders use Divvy bikes differently?
  2. Why would casual riders buy Divvy annual memberships?
  3. How can Divvy use digital media to influence casual riders to become members?

Graphs from RStudio

  1. What is your final conclusion based on your analysis?
    • In conclusion, according to the data Cyclistic has a strong userbase that tend to use our bike share program to get from point A to point B. Our members follow a seasonal riding pattern due to cold winter months. However, although lower, the turn out is consistent over the winter months, unlike our casual riders who tend disapper during from november to march.
    • This isn't a big surprise as Chicago has a seasonal tourism industry which tends to die down in late fall and winter and starts picking up in the spring. I would suggest that for our casual customers we have 2 set of rates during the year. From start of march to end of october we have one set of rates and from november to february end we lower our rates so to increase income during the winter months.
    • Casual riders tend to ride there bike for FOUR TIMES longer than our members. Implemeting a higher cost per mintue at a lower time allotment for single tirps than our current pricing would incentivise casual riders that buy multiple single trip and or day trip pass over a course of a month. Currently our pricing for a single trip is:
      $3.30/30 mintues + 0.15 per extra minute
    • We should lower either lower the time limit for the single trip to 20 minutes or increase the cost per minute to: $0.30 extra per minute.
    • So the riders see the benefit of getting a membership than paying for multiple single trips over a week/month. Having additional information like distance a bike is riden per trip would help make decisions more concrete. Knowing how and where the bikes are being riden the most during the year can help us lot more bike stands in those areas and much much more.
